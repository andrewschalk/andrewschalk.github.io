Why do I have a user agreement and privacy policy?

First off, good job on clicking on the user agreement or privacy policy. But what if you hadn’t? These are satire (if that wasn’t obvious) and in no way enforceable or reasonable. However, I could have put something that was an actual agreement with the same power as all the other agreements at the bottom of pages across the web.

What I’m trying to do here, besides just being silly, is highlight the ridiculousness of the user agreements across the web. Many of these policies contain writing almost as ridiculous and just as unenforceable as what’s in my policies. By using their website, you may be agreeing to things that you would never normally agree to. It is also not uncommon to add clauses in these agreements that have no basis in anything legal (until someone attempts to enforce it, you can say a lot of crazy things). So, even if you actually clicked on the link to view it, had time to read the thousands of words, and could understand the complicated legalese, you’d probably still end up thinking that these ridiculous things are enforceable or legal. Hopefully, I’ve got you thinking a little more about these agreements that we all mindlessly let slip by without a second thought.
